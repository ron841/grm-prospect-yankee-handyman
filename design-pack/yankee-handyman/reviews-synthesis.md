## 0. Why this page exists

Anthony has 50 Google reviews at 4.9 stars; 44 carry body text; 43 carry owner replies. Reviews are his single strongest asset class. The homepage proof section uses 3 of them — that's right for the homepage container, wrong as the only surface. This page is where the asset class lives at full weight.

**Architectural decision (locked iter-2 close):** dedicated reviews page when reviews are the strongest asset class for a prospect. Per-prospect judgment. For Anthony: yes.

## 1. Voice argument

The reviews page is **customer-voice forward, owner-voice supporting**. Customer reviews carry the testimonial weight; Anthony's replies are the trust frame around them.

Reply-voice register (from corpus analysis):
- Length: 8 canned (<30 chars), 28 short (30-100), 7 medium (100-250), 0 detailed. Caps tight.
- Gratitude-first openers ("Thank you for choosing the Yankee Handyman", "It was my pleasure")
- Family/community framing ("being a part of the family", "we appreciate our community")
- Forward-looking ("looking forward to more projects", "I'm sure we will meet again")
- Emoji light (5 replies use 😁)
- "We"/"I/me" oscillation — solo-operator with collective framing. **Leave it.** Forced consistency falsifies.

**Authoring discipline:** any synthesis-authored connector copy on this page caps at 100 chars. Match Anthony's tempo, don't write prose around his quotes.

**Verbatim phrases worth lifting** for connector copy / section breaks:
- "being a part of the family"
- "It was my pleasure"
- "looking forward to more projects"
- "Thank you for trusting us"

## 2. Three-layer page structure

1. **Curated leads (top, 5 review/reply pairs)** — strongest evidence, paired inline. No "see reply" disclosure; the reply IS the evidence.
2. **One 1-star slot** (between curated leads and grouped sections) — DM_'s review and Anthony's reply, side-by-side, no editorial wrapper. Plain header.
3. **Grouped by service type (middle, ~30 pairs)** — group order by job-noun frequency from corpus. Within group: by review length (longer first; depth context).
4. **Archive (bottom)** — date-ordered remaining reviews not surfaced above.

No pagination. Single page at 44.

## 3. Curation rules (locked iter-2 close)

Applied in order of priority:

1. **Specificity > generic praise.** "Fixed the disposal in twenty minutes" beats "great service." Length is a proxy; specificity is the real signal.
2. **Reply-as-evidence > reply-as-courtesy.** Reviews where Anthony's reply adds concrete trade detail or named-customer warmth ("Thank you Judy", "It was a mess now it's a success") outrank reviews of equivalent length whose replies are generic ("Thank you", "Your very welcome").
3. **Competitor-mention reviews carry differentiator weight on top of testimonial weight.** Surface prominently. Cheryl Mulhern ("plumbers who quoted $350-$500"), Diane Bennett ("everyone said yes or they would have charged almost $1000 more"), Devin P ("a quarter of what we were quoted elsewhere"), Linda Weis ("went through 10 contractors and no one showed up").
4. **Repeat-customer language outranks first-time praise.** Judy ("we keep him on speed dial"), Andrew Abad ("over 20 projects for me"), audrey bittman ("several years"), JEAN JONES ("recommended him to at least four other people").
5. **Named-project specificity outranks abstract competence.** "Pool deck with sanded edges" beats "great work."

## 5. The 1-star slot

Position: between curated leads and grouped sections. Not in archive.

Frame: **plain header. No editorial wrapper.** Header reads "April 2024" or "One 1-star review." Trust the reader.

Pairing: DM_'s review and Anthony's reply, side-by-side, full text both. No truncation. The reply (*"I'm sorry to hear we weren't on the same page. I take pride in the quality of my work and aim for 5-star service with every client. I appreciate your feedback. Best of luck with your future home projects."*) is the trust artifact. Let it carry.

This slot is worth more than ten 5-star reviews stacked.
